    THE LITTLE MEN WHO LIVED IN MY BEARD

    By Jeffrey Goldman

    The last time I saw her alive was on an uncommonly hot day in October. We'd met at the usual place: a little patch of grass facing the river behind Best Buy. Her text simply read, “We need to talk.” The thermostat was pushing 80 and the armpits of my t-shirt were horribly stained. She had worn a white T-shirt, jean shorts, pink flip flops and a wool scarf with a pattern consisting of four reindeer riding a roller coaster with more corkscrews and loops than I would ever dare to ride. Where she’d bought such a scarf, and why she’d worn it in that blazing heat, I'll never know. Not for lack of asking, mind you, but because she wasn't sure herself.

    “Does it matter?” A pile of knotted dandelions stems was growing at her bare feet. She’d walked around in the water and left her flip-flops to dry by the river’s edge.

    “No, not really.”

    “Good.”
